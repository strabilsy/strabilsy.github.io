About Me

Hi, my name is Samier Trabilsy. I’m an aspiring programmer from Queens, NY and I am currently a student studying Computer Science at Stony Brook University. I take my education very seriously and work fairly hard in school not just for good grades, but also for the sake of learning and expanding the boundaries of my knowledge. At the same time, I consider myself to be a pretty outgoing guy. I love to joke around with my friends and family and if you get to know me, you’ll be quick to find that I don’t take a lot of things too seriously. My hobbies include playing soccer and basketball, playing video games, and generally just hanging out with my friends. I’m also an avid fan of the New York Knicks.

Motivation

I love technology, especially the tech I use in my everyday life: my Android phone, my MacBook, my Xbox 360, etc. However, I realize "technology" is much more than just these consumer electronics. Technology has the power to solve real-world problems and to make significant impacts on the well being of billions of individuals. I believe its bounds are endless‐-it can bring clean water to impoverished areas, create renewable, sustainable energy sources that preserve the environment, or even provide adequate education for every child in the world. The infinite potential I see in technology is what motivates me to pursue a career in Computer Science. I find it extremely fascinating that with a strong understanding of programming, sound logic, and a little creativity, one can find a solution to practically anything! I hope to use the knowledge and tools I acquire in this field to create something that improves the world around me. Although I’m not quite sure what exact job I would like to be doing 10 years from now, if I can somehow make a positive impact on the lives of the people around me, I would feel accomplished knowing I made a difference.
